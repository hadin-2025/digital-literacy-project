**Case Study: UPI/Online Payment Fraud**

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a type of digital financial crime in which scammers trick users into transferring money or revealing sensitive banking details. With the rapid growth of digital payments, such frauds have become increasingly common.

Typically, the fraud begins when a scammer contacts the victim via phone call, SMS, or messaging apps, pretending to be a bank official, customer care executive, or even a buyer/seller. In the first step, they create a sense of urgency or trust. Next, they ask the victim to share confidential information such as OTPs, UPI PINs, or request them to click on a malicious link. In many cases, the fraudster sends a “collect request” on a UPI app and convinces the victim that approving it will result in receiving money, when in reality it deducts money from their account. Once the victim authorizes the transaction, the money is instantly transferred to the scammer’s account, making recovery difficult.

The most common targets are elderly individuals, new digital users, small business owners, and people unfamiliar with online security practices. However, even educated users can fall victim due to panic or lack of awareness.

The consequences include financial loss, emotional stress, and loss of trust in digital systems. In severe cases, victims may lose their entire savings. This highlights the importance of digital literacy, awareness, and cautious behavior while using online payment platforms.
